Carbon Emission in Anthropocene

Introduction

The fact that we are facing currently is the total anthropogenic GHG emissions have continued to increase over 1970 to 2020 with larger absolute decadal increases toward the end of this period. Carbon emissions from fossil fuel combustion and industrial processes contributed about 78% of the total GHG emission increase from 1970 to 2020, with a similar percentage contribution for the period 2000â€“2010. At the same time, various investigations have shown that the overall costs of climate change damage are significant and are expected to increase over time.So, action should be done under the pressure of the necessity. Without additional efforts to reduce GHG emissions beyond those in place today, emissions growth is expected to persist driven by growth in global population and economic activities.

In this project, we plan to show the changes and patterns of global carbon emissions through a multi-dimensional visualization method, and to provide decision support for subsequent emission reduction strategies by summarizing past carbon emission patterns. First, we show the time-series changes in carbon emissions over the past 60 years across the world, exploring the differences in carbon emissions patterns between developed and developing countries. We then wish to discuss, in terms of the time series of historical changes in carbon emissions, the positive or negative impact of some major historical events on changes in carbon emission. Thirdly, we use the COVID-19 epidemic as a particular historical event to observe how human public policies and human activities have influenced carbon emissions at the regional scale. Finally, we turn our attention to the carbon trading system, focusing on the current international carbon market and carbon trading.
